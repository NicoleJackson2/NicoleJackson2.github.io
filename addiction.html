Addiction

What is this?

This category is about addiction support and mentions all standards which relate to this, including where to get help with this.

There are standards which relate to this category.

These are:

  • Standard 1: Same Day Access
  • Standard 2: Choice
  • Standard 3: Assertive Outreach and Anticipatory Care
  • Standard 7: Primary care

Standard 1: Same Day Access

What is it?

This standard is about getting access to medication immediately instead of having to wait weeks, or possibly months to start.

How does this help with the category?

This helps with addiction as it allows the person to get medication the same day they need it instead of waiting, which could potentially stop them from taking dangerous substances instead.

Where to get help?

To get help with this you can contact your doctor, the NHS or social workers.

Standard 2: Choice

What is it?

This standard is about a person being able to decide their own medication and dosage, after discussing it with a worker. It also allows them to make changes to the medication if cirucmstances change.

How does this help with the category?

This helps with addiction as it allowes the person to make their own decisions on their medication and dosage with the help of a professional, helping them to better understand their medication and take control over their own choices.

Where to get help?

To get help with this you can contact your doctor, the NHS or any social workers you have.

Standard 3: Assertive Outreach and Anticipatory Care

What is it?

This standard is about providing support to those who are thought to be high risk due to drug use.

How does this help with the category?

This helps with addiction by providing support to those who need it. By contacting someone at high risk, it could be potentially life saving.

Where to get help?

If you think someone is at high risk and could benefit from this you can contact your local council, or any support workers.

Standard 7: Primary Care

What is it?

This standard allows people to access their medication or support through priamry cares providers such as GP'S or community pharmacy.

How does this help with the category?

This helps with addiction as it makes it easier for people to get access to their medication/treatment by being able to use primary care providers for this. It also makes treatment more seamless for the person as it helps create a partnership between all health and social care areas.

Where to get help?

To get help with this you can contact your doctor or social workers.
