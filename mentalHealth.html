Mental Health

What is this?-+

This category is about mental health support and mentions all standards which relate to this, including where to get help with this.

There are standards which relate to this category.

These are:

  • Standard 3: Assertive Outreach and Anticipatory Care
  • Standard 5: Retention
  • Standard 6: Psychological Support
  • Standard 9: Mental Health
  • Standard 10: Trauma Informed care

Standard 3: Assertive Outreach and Anticipatory Care

What is it?

This standard is about providing support to those who are thought to be high risk due to drug use.

How does this help with the category?

This helps with mental health as the person who is high risk could also be struggling with mental health issues and this would allow them to get help.

Where to get help?

If you think someone is at high risk and could benefit from this you can contact your local council, or any support workers.

Standard 5: Retention

What is it?

This standard allows people to stay in treatment for as long as they want, as well as at key points in their life such as leaving hospital or prison.

How does this help with the category?

This helps with mental health as it takes away the stress of not knowing how long they can be in treatment by allowing them to stay as long as they need.

Where to get help?

To get help with this you can contact social workers or the NHS.

Standard 6: Psychological Support

What is it?

This standard is about highlighting the role that positive relationships play by supporting people to develop positive relationships and new ways of coping.

How does this help with the category?

This helps with mental health as it helps the person to build new coping mechanisms and positive relationships to better their mental health.

Where to get help?

To get help with this you can contact social workers or NHS.

Standard 9: Mental Health

What is it?

This standard allows people to have mental health treatment alongside their drug treatment.

How does this help with the category?

This helps with mental health as it allows people to have access to professional mental health treatment.

Where to get help?

To get help with this you can contact your doctor, the NHS or your social worker.

Standard 10: Trauma Informed Care

What is it?

This standard is to recognise that people in treatment for drug related issues may have experienced trauma which could still be impacting them.

How does this help with the category?

This helps with mental health as it recognises trauma that people may have faced and that this could be impacting their mental health, so will provide support for this.

Where to get help?

To get help with this you can contact your doctor, the NHS, or your social worker.
