MAT Standards in Scotland

What are they? -+

MAT standards stands for Medication Assisted Treatment standards. This is a set of 10 standards within Scotland that was created in 2021 to help reduce deaths and other harms, and promote recovery from drug-related issues. These standards are a framework that makes sure MAT is provided in a safe, effective and accessible way to those who need it, it allows drug treatment to be safe and accessible across Scotland. The standards help to enable people to make an informed choice about care. These standards apply to all services and organisations in Scotland that deliver care.

Who do they help?-+

The standards are there to help anyone affected by drug-related issues, including people in treatment, their families or friends. It is also there to help services and organisations that support these people to make sure they are giving the correct support.

Standards

Standard 1: Same Day Access

Standard 2: Choice

Standard 3: Assertive Outreach and Anticipatory Care

Standard 4: Harm Reduction

Standard 5: Retention

Standard 6: Psychological Support

Standard 7: Primary Care

Standard 8: Independent Advocacy and Social Support

Standard 9: Mental Health

Standard 10: Trauma Informed Care
